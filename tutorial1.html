Problem Set

Q1 Direct Proof

Prove that the sum of two even integers is always even.

Q2 Proof by Contradiction

Prove that \( \sqrt{2} \) is irrational. Generalize the proof for any prime \( p \) to say \( \sqrt{p} \) is irrational.

Q3 Proof by Contrapositive

Prove that if \( n^2 \) is even, then \( n \) is even, where \( n \) is an integer.

Q4 Mathematical Induction

Prove by induction that for all positive integers \( n \):

\[ 1 + 2 + 3 + \cdots + n = \frac{n(n+1)}{2} \]

Generalize the technique of proof for any series \( \sum_{i=1}^{n} i^k = f(n) \). Find a closed form formula for \( f(n) \). Hint: Use binomial expansion.

Q5 Pythagorean Triplets — Three Methods

A Pythagorean triplet is a set of three positive integers \( (a, b, c) \) such that \( a^2 + b^2 = c^2 \). Prove that there are infinitely many Pythagorean triplets using three different methods.

Q6 Proof by Cases

Prove that \( n^2 + n \) is even for all integers \( n \). What would be a function that says \( f(n) \) is divisible by a prime \( p \) for all integers \( n \in \mathbb{N} \)?

Q7 Existence Proof

Prove that there exist irrational numbers \( a \) and \( b \) such that \( a^b \) is rational.

Q8 Divisibility

Prove that for any integer \( n \), the number \( n^3 - n \) is divisible by \( 6 \). What is a function \( f(n) \) that is divisible by \( k \)?

Q9 Bogus Proof

Prove that Arithmetic Mean \( \ge \) Geometric Mean.

$$ \begin{aligned} \dfrac{a+b}{2} &\ge \sqrt{ab} \\ a+b &\ge 2\sqrt{ab} \\ a^2 + 2ab + b^2 &\ge 4ab \\ a^2 - 2ab + b^2 &\ge 0 \\ (a-b)^2 &\ge 0 \end{aligned} $$

The last statement is true because \( a-b \) is a real number, and the square of a real number is never negative. This proves the claim.

In the above proof, identify what is wrong.

Q10 Uniqueness Proof

Prove that if \( x^2 = 4 \) and \( x > 0 \), then \( x = 2 \) is the unique solution.

Logical Equivalences

Q11

Show that \( \neg(p \vee (\neg p \wedge q)) \) and \( \neg p \wedge \neg q \) are logically equivalent by developing a series of logical equivalences.

Q12

Prove that \( (p \rightarrow q) \wedge (p \rightarrow r) \) and \( p \rightarrow (q \wedge r) \) are logically equivalent using logical equivalence laws.

Q13

Show that \( \neg(p \rightarrow q) \) and \( p \wedge \neg q \) are logically equivalent by applying logical equivalence transformations.

Q14

Prove that \( (p \vee q) \wedge (\neg p \vee r) \) and \( (p \wedge r) \vee (q \wedge \neg p) \vee (q \wedge r) \) are logically equivalent using the distributive laws.

Q15

Show that \( \neg((p \vee q) \wedge (\neg p \vee \neg q)) \) and \( \neg p \wedge \neg q \) are logically equivalent by developing a series of logical equivalences.

Q16

Prove that \( (p \rightarrow q) \vee (q \rightarrow p) \) is a tautology using logical equivalences.

Q17

Show that \( \neg(p \wedge (q \vee \neg r)) \) and \( \neg p \vee (\neg q \wedge r) \) are logically equivalent by applying De Morgan's laws and other logical equivalences.

Q18

Prove that \( (p \rightarrow q) \wedge (q \rightarrow r) \) and \( (p \rightarrow r) \wedge ((p \wedge \neg r) \rightarrow \neg q) \) are logically equivalent.

Q19

Show that \( ((p \vee q) \rightarrow r) \) and \( (p \rightarrow r) \wedge (q \rightarrow r) \) are logically equivalent using logical equivalence laws.

Q20

Prove that \( \neg(p \leftrightarrow q) \) and \( (p \wedge \neg q) \vee (\neg p \wedge q) \) are logically equivalent by developing a series of logical equivalences.
